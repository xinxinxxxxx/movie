Traditional Documentary VS Interactive Documentary

  • The traditional documentary narrative mode is relatively homogeneous. They establish a physical distance between the viewer and the content, thus forming a passive viewing experience (Zhang & Zhou, 901). Traditional documentaries mainly rely on devices, such as television and smart phones, which passively attract the audience within the narrative framework of the director, limiting their active participation (Zhang & Zhou, 902).

    E LIVING PLANET （1984）

  • The linear structure of traditional documentary further limits the audience's choice and control ability. In contrast, the need for physical interaction increases viewer interest and engagement, characteristics that are lacking in traditional documentaries (Charles). As a result, linear documentaries can seem tedious to audiences accustomed to freedom and interaction, leading to selective rejection.
