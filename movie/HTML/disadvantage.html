Disadvantages of interactive documentaries

Lack of transparency

Although interactive documentaries have already changed many limitations of traditional documentaries, it is still not perfect. It still has limitations, such as lack of transparency in length and content, which can lead to lower completion rates and limited freedom of choice for users in interactive documentaries.The length of interactive documentaries is not explicitly stated in numbers, so audiences cannot quickly assess how long the entire viewing experience will take. This may ultimately lead to audiences skipping parts of the content or choosing not to watch at all (Charles).

Exploration in this interactive documentary (The world in the blocks) involves visual, auditory and behavioural actions such as clicking and swiping. The fixed sequence and pattern of content makes the viewer feel that they are going through some information. There is no sense of immersion. Therefore, although this kind of interactive documentary can follow the guide and understand the story completely, it lacks a certain degree of freedom of exploration. It will also lead to the loss of viewers and the inability to catch the attention of the audience.

Bear 7

This documentary, on the other hand, is just the opposite of the above example. Free to explore, the pages of this interactive documentary (Bear 71) are largely unguided. The viewer can drag, slide and click on the map in the page to find the story content. This can lead to an inability to understand the end of the story or the central idea if something is not found.
