Storming Juno

immersive By stimulating the different senses of human beings, the purpose of attracting attention is achieved.

  • Storming Juno is an interactive documentary on the Normandy landings detailing the historic events of 6 June 1944, when Canadian troops landed on Juno Beach. It uses a variety of interactive elements to give viewers insight into this pivotal moment in history. Viewers can choose from different character perspectives, such as infantry, paratroopers and tank commanders, to experience the different tasks and challenges they faced during the battle.

  • Detailed interactive maps are provided in the documentary, allowing viewers to click on different locations on the map to view relevant historical events, tactical layouts, and battle scenarios. The documentary contains numerous historical video clips, battle recordings and survivor interviews that viewers can click on to watch and listen to for in-depth insight into every detail. The director also uses modern technology to re-enact battle scenes, allowing viewers to interact and understand the context and impact of each tactical decision.

Bear 71

Add multiple interactive modes A map that can be explored at will

  • Created by Leanne Allison and Jeremy Mendes, Bear 71 explores the relationship between technology, wildlife and humans. Through a unique interactive format, the documentary tells the story of a female grizzly bear named Bear 71, who lived in Canada's Banff National Park and was tracked by wildlife managers for eight years.

  • Viewers can track the movements of Bear 71 and other wildlife in real time through a dynamic interactive map. The map shows the animals' living areas and their intersection with the human world. Different marked points on the map provide rich multimedia content, including video clips, audio recordings and images,
